Points to keep in mind

What breed of dog is right for me?

The wide variety of dog breeds means there is a suitable dog for almost everyone. However, all breeds have very different needs, so it's important to base your choice on lifestyle rather than appearance. For example, Siberian Huskies are bred to run, so they are alone in the apartment all day, and in the evening to the store he does not like to walk ten minutes. Many purebred dogs are also prone to certain health issues that need to be carefully researched before choosing.

Can I afford a dog?

Dogs are expensive. Initial acquisition or acquisition cost is just the beginning. They have to be fed, dewormed and vaccinated on a standard basis, and accidents and illnesses can cost more. Your dog can be with you for 15 years and older dogs like ours often need medication. Even when purchasing, the dog's overall cost of living is an important consideration.

Always check temperament of puppy's parent.

Meet the puppy's parents and check their temperament. Remember that temperament is genetic. Parents' temperaments are often passed on to puppies.

Diet

Your dog's diet affects his health, happiness and his behaviour. So many issues that affect some pooches such as itchy skin, loose stools and lethargy might be eliminated entirely by simply feeding them a more natural diet containing ingredients that actually benefit them.

Exercise

  • mental health

    • Upbringing

    Dogs form attachments to their human parents in the same way, so in order for them to grow into emotionally stable adult dogs, they need healthy interaction. A parent's emotional state affects dogs the same way it affects children. An important factor in the emotional development of dogs is how warm a caregiver is. A parent who is depressed or stressed affects a dog no differently than a child.

    Training

    Training motivates dogs and rewards good behavior. You can build a bond with your dog based on mutual trust and respect, not fear or intimidation. Positively trained dogs are more patient, self-disciplined, and behave consistently in a variety of situations.

    • Gromming

    When most people think of pet grooming, images of grinding, brushing and chasing pets come to mind. Some people have the misconception that animals don't need to be cared for. However, it is an important aspect of your pet's overall well-being and health. Pet care is also important for the whole family.

    You don't want your dirty, smelly pet jumping on your couch or your clean sheets. Proper pet care can eliminate shedding, fleas, ticks, and a variety of other health problems. Proper grooming is more than just having a cute pet. Grooming your pet also addresses potentially unhealthy conditions. It can also help identify underlying medical conditions and diseases early. So you can bring your hairy friend to therapy faster and more efficiently.
